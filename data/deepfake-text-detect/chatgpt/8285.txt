I don't particularly hold this view dearly, I just expect there might be some budding historians on Reddit with more information! It was influenced mostly by [this article] , which has a lot of interesting reading about why the commonly held belief is incorrect. The landing gear did not fold in later versions - it had 5 wheels instead (although that's extremely close to folding into 3!). And if you're wondering how they managed weight distribution for such large aircraft: at least one version used an engine-powered rotor mounted above each wingtip and driven via shafts linked together through gears so as all tips were 'equally' powered...
