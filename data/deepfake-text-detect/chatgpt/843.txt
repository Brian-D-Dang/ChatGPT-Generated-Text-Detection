This is the case with "relapse prevention" programs, which use medication, supportive treatment, and other interventions to increase the chance of a person quitting a drug. In fact, most people who have a drug addiction spontaneously kick it, without the need for medical treatment, a study has found. This is because of the way the brain responds to a drug, and the symptoms that a person experiences when they are high can be seen even in the absence of a substance. The researchers, led by Dr. Adam Barrett, a psychiatrist at Johns Hopkins, monitored the brain activity of people with a history of substance abuse. Barrett found that there were three chemical reactions that the brain uses to control the reward system: dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin.
