Cigarettes are the leading cause of preventable death, responsible for roughly 480,000 deaths per year in the US. 41,000 of those deaths aren't even those of users they are the result of secondhand smoke. While most people are aware of the dangers of smoking, many are unaware of the effects of secondhand smoke. Secondhand smoke is a toxic combination of the smoke breathed out by smokers and the smoke released by the burning end of a cigarette. Secondhand smoke is a problem for non-smokers in the household, in the workplace and in public places. Toxic chemicals in secondhand smoke include tar, nicotine, carbon monoxide and cyanide. There are more than 4,000 chemicals in secondhand smoke. These chemicals are dangerous to the people inhaling the smoke and also those who are exposed to the smoke. Children exposed to secondhand smoke are at risk of developing asthma. Exposure to secondhand smoke can also lead to ear infections and pneumonia, decreased lung function, coughing and wheezing and bronchitis. Children exposed to secondhand smoke are also more likely to develop lung cancer and heart disease. Infants and young children are particularly vulnerable to the effects of secondhand smoke. Infants exposed to secondhand smoke are at greater risk of Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS). Exposure to secondhand smoke also affects the development of babies. Secondhand smoke may also cause cancer in non-smokers. Lung cancer is the most common type of cancer in the US and is linked to secondhand smoke. Lung cancer is responsible for 29 of all cancer deaths. Some of the chemicals in secondhand smoke may cause cancer. There is also a link between secondhand smoke and stomach cancer. Studies have shown that babies exposed to secondhand smoke are more likely to have a low birth weight. Babies that are born to mothers that are exposed to secondhand smoke may be born early, at a lower birth weight and are at a greater risk of death. Secondhand smoke exposure may also lead to a variety of other health problems. Secondhand smoke may cause heart disease and stroke in non-smokers. Many people who are exposed to secondhand smoke are unable to quit smoking. If you are a smoker, it is important to quit smoking and to make sure that you don't expose your family to secondhand smoke.
