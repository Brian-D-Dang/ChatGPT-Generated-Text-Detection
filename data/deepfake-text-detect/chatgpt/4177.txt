After every school shooting the debate gun violence in schools starts up with one side supporting reduced gun rights and the other side supporting banning violent video games. Whenever the topic of guns is brought up, I want to know what people are actually saying about them at this point? I heard a guest on NPR's Sunday Morning Futures program two weeks ago who had done some research into exactly why there has been so little shootings in American public high schools as opposed to a convention center or a movie theater. And while she couldn't determine it outright, she concluded that it has something to do with the fact that most kids just don't have access to these weapons because they live in places where such devices aren't readily available. This is hardly surprising considering that the average age of first time gun users was only 11 years old when he first owned a firearm. She also mentioned how kids were arming themselves more often than ever before due to fears associated with the high rate of suicide among teens today (and especially young men).
