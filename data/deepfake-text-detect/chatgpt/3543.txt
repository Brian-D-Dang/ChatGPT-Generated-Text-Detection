Marriage as a word comes from the Middle english word mariage, which according to sources from Wikipedia, comes from around CE. Word comes from another Old French word then to Latin word maritare, which means "to marry" or "to connect" two people. The term has evolved over time and has different interpretations and meanings in different cultures and societies. In most Western cultures, marriage is a legal and social union between two individuals, often recognized by law, which establishes rights and obligations between them, as well as between them and their children, if any.
