Milton Friedman on Mises: "I don't agree with everything that Ludwig von Mises said or wrote; I'm sure that most people who have ever read anything by any Nobel Prize winner do not feel they are going along entirely with what every word of this person says. But when one looks at all that Mises has written and produced over a period of time it becomes clear that he did understand very well many things which nobody else seemed able to grasp for a long time... The reason why I like so much of what Mises' writes suggests to me that we may look upon his ideas as an example of how someone can be both as practical as possible and yet keep their eyes open to important broader issues that need fuller consideration..."
