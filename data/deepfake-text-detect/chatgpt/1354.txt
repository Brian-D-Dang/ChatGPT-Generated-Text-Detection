The fact that men are disadvantaged in child custody cases is appalling. So is that fact that men who call the police to report domestic violence against them are more likely than their female counterparts to be arrested and charged with a crime, according to data from the state attorney general's office reviewed by The Times. "I'm just so tired of doing this," said Theresa Lewis-Ortiz, an organizer for California Families United who has been meeting with lawmakers since January as they consider legislation aimed at eliminating gender discrimination in divorce proceedings. "It seems like there were never any women involved when it comes to making these laws."
