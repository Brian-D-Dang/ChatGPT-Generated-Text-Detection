Currently, [it is a crime to lie to any federal officer.] It is an astoundingly severe crime too, carrying a maximum sentence up to five years in prison. Under the federal criminal code, the offense is referred to as "making false statements" and is defined as any false or fraudulent statement made by an individual in a matter within the jurisdiction of the federal government. This includes lying to officers of the FBI, IRS, Department of Homeland Security, Secret Service, Customs and Border Protection, and more. The intent of the law is to prevent individuals from attempting to mislead or obstruct justice by providing false information.
