Nuclear weapons are a nearly surefire way to prevent aggression from other state actorsespecially if the aggressor state also possesses nukes. So, during the division of the Soviet Union, the problem arose about what to do with the nuclear weapons spread across the Soviet Republics. I want to focus on the stockpiles in Ukraine specifically because of the obviously contentious air surrounding Russo-Ukrainian relations as of late. I argue that the government of Ukraine should have retained its Soviet stockpile of nuclear weapons upon independence, and in doing so, would have prevented military aggression by Russiaformally, as is believed by many, or informally, as is believed by othersinto Eastern Ukraine or even Crimea.
