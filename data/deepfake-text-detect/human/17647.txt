While much has been made on reddit about dropping the 'ng' to a simple 'n' at the end of -ing words (e.g. working workin', etc), I haven't been able to find much about the reverse: people overpronouncing the g as a hard 'g' sound (as in 'glossy' or 'gamble. There are a few people I'm in contact with who, in their everyday speech, will say things like "I've been workinGUH on a new project to refine the ability of our software to ...." or "I've been thinkinGH lately about a new way to approach..." It drives me absolutely bonkers, because these are otherwise educated and informed individuals who have good paying jobs and speak to a lot of other people daily as part of those jobs, and I think they sound like second-graders with runny noses. Is there a part of the country where this is acceptable? (I'm from the midwest, and my teachers tried to stamp this out when I was in elementary school.)
