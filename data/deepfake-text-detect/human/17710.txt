I believe that if someone is not above a certain academic threshold by say, 13, then we should not force them to do tests that they will obviously fail, or even worse do tests that are nearer their actual abilities as to make them pale in comparison to other, more able, students. Instead I think we should teach them a trade that had a more linear career path into a job. We currently have, in the UK, around [20 Unemployment of 16 to 24 year olds.] Why is this? In my opinion this is partly due, not down to the fact that people have no qualifications, but down to the fact they have the wrong ones. They have qualifications that don't apply to any realistic line of work for them. In conclusion, I believe that teaching kids a trade (i.e Bricklayer, Carpenter, Electrician, Mechanic etc.) would greatly benefit both them and society.
