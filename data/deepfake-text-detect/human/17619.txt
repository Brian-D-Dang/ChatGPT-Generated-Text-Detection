I read a compelling article by Victor Davis Hanson in the City Jounral the other day, which talked about the history of the water projects in central California in the early and mid-twentieth century. Hanson points the finger specifically at the environmentalists who derailed the Klamath River diversion project in the 70s, which would have provided enough water to supply San Francisco alone for thirty years.
